# about

I'm Praveen — an engineering team lead at Accelo, the service operations platform used by thousands of consultancies and agencies worldwide to quote, deliver, and bill work without the usual chaos.

Over 8+ years at Accelo I've grown from writing Perl unit tests to leading the team that rebuilt our platform end-to-end as v4 — taking it from inception to public beta. I work across a full-stack of GraphQL APIs (Apollo + Nexus, code-first), a Next.js web app, and a Perl backend, and most recently helped integrate a newly acquired AI-powered platform into our ecosystem.

Before Accelo I cut my teeth at Wipro in India and earlier at G-TEC, giving me roughly a decade in software all up. I migrated to Australia in 2016 for a Master of Computer Science at the University of Wollongong, stayed, and became an Australian citizen along the way.

Off the clock: we recently built our own home in Calderwood, NSW (moved in December 2025), I travel whenever I can, and I'm married to Elna since 2021. Originally from Ezhacherry, Kerala, India — a true Malayali at heart.

# hobbies & interests

Mostly travel and good food. Some books, some cricket, the occasional hike.

🗺️

Travel

all 6 states + ACT

I've made it to every Australian state and territory and keep going back for more. In Western Australia, Rottnest Island and its quokkas — and some of the most beautiful beaches I've seen. In South Australia, the wineries of the Barossa Valley and the Fleurieu Peninsula, plus the best sunset of my life at Sellicks Beach. The tropical landscapes of Cairns and the Great Barrier Reef in Queensland. The Opera House and a cruise across the Tasman Sea on the NSW coast. Hobart's Mount Wellington and the old convict colony at Port Arthur in Tasmania. The opal-mining town of Lightning Ridge in outback NSW. Canberra at Floriade time — Parliament House, the Royal Australian Mint, the lot.

I drive a lot. The Great Ocean Road to the Twelve Apostles in Victoria and the Indian Ocean Drive in WA are two of my favourites. The longest day I've ever done behind the wheel was 1,350 km from Wollongong to Adelaide in a single sitting. And the most memorable was solo from Broken Hill to Adelaide through a dust storm — a hell of an adventure I won't forget.
